I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Verifiable Robotics Research Group in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Cornell University. I am advised by Prof. Hadas Kress-Gazit. I am interested in the synthesis of provably-correct controllers for dynamical systems and the automatic discovery of specification revisions for guaranteeing execution in dynamic environments. My work draws from a broad spectrum of ideas, including optimization, hybrid systems, formal methods, and control systems.

Prior to Cornell and after receiving my B.S. and M.S. degrees at Virginia Tech, I spent several years in industry as an aerospace engineer at NASA and Impact Tech (now Sikorsky). While at NASA, I was part of the team that developed the software tool C-MAPSS, winner of a Space Act Award and a NASA Group Achievement Award.
